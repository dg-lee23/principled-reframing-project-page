Existing motion transfer methods can replicate the given motion, but are constrained by fixed framing. Therefore, we propose a principled reframing approach that can be applied on top of them. Our method enables control over object position, camera zoom, and perspective.

Abstract

Video motion transfer methods have demonstrated remarkable performance in generating new videos with the given text prompt while replicating the object's motion in a reference video. However, they inherently retain the original framing (i.e., object position, zooming, perspective), limiting their applicability. To address this challenge, we investigate the feasibility of reframing in motion transfer using video diffusion models. Specifically, we analyze the categories of existing motion transfer methods, assess their reframing capabilities, and propose a general approach that utilizes parametric global warping to provide effective framing control in these methods. Our analyses showed that applying transformations to feature vectors, which contain rich semantic information, can support such control over framing. With a seamless integration of reframing into motion transfer methods, high-quality, reframed videos that can even handle unseen areas in the original reference frame can be produced. Experiments show that our approach enables reframing across diverse motion transfer methods while preserving motion consistency and generation quality with minimal computational overhead.

Results with Reframing

We present results of using reframing for object shifting, camera zoom adjustments, and perspective shifts.
